HIV Awareness

Introduction

Welcome to our HIV Awareness learning page. Here, you'll find clear, accurate information about HIV, how it's transmitted, how to protect yourself and others, and what it means to live with HIV today. Our goal is to empower you with knowledge, dispel myths, and foster a supportive community. Understanding HIV is the first step towards prevention, reducing stigma, and ensuring everyone has access to the care and support they need to live healthy lives.

What is HIV?

  • HIV stands for Human Immunodeficiency Virus. It's a virus that attacks the body's immune system.

  • The immune system helps the body fight off infections and diseases. HIV targets and destroys CD4 cells (T-cells), a type of white blood cell that plays a crucial role in immune function.

  • Over time, if HIV is not treated, the damage to the immune system can lead to Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS). AIDS is a set of symptoms and illnesses that occur at the late stage of HIV infection when the immune system is severely damaged.

  • Important: With early diagnosis and consistent treatment, people with HIV can prevent the progression to AIDS and live long, healthy lives.

How is HIV Transmitted?

    HIV can be transmitted through specific bodily fluids from an infected person that come into contact with a mucous membrane (such as inside the rectum, vagina, penis, or mouth), damaged tissue, or are directly injected into the bloodstream. These fluids include:

  • Blood

  • Semen and pre-seminal fluid

  • Rectal fluids

  • Vaginal fluids

  • Breast milk

HIV is NOT transmitted through:

  • Air or water

  • Saliva, sweat, or tears (if not mixed with blood)

  • Insects, including mosquitoes

  • Sharing food or drinks

  • Hugging, shaking hands, or casual contact

  • Using the same toilets or showers
