ABOUT HIV/AIDS

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that attacks the immune system,
specifically targeting CD4 cells (a type of white blood cell), weakening the body's ability to fight infections and diseases. If untreated, it can progress to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome),
a severe stage of the infection.

As of the end of 2024, an estimated 40.8 million people globally were living with HIV,
with 65% of cases in the WHO African Region. Despite its impact, antiretroviral therapy (ART) can effectively manage the virus, allowing individuals to live healthy lives and reducing transmission risks.
