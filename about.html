The Red Ribbon Initiative was launched in 1991 by a group of artists
in New York as a symbol of compassion and support for people living with HIV and AIDS.
It quickly became an international emblem of HIV awareness, solidarity,
and the fight against stigma and discrimination.
The red color signifies love, passion, and the blood shared by all people,
and the ribbon serves as a reminder of the importance of education, prevention,
and support for those affected by HIV/AIDS. It is now globally recognized,
especially during World AIDS Day on December 1st.
